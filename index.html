Perk up your Financial Habit

Earn Your Own Allowance

Regardless of your age, one of the most important lessons you have to learn is that money is a finite resource. When you have to work for your own money—as your parents likely do—you’ll learn to use it more carefully.

Explore

Have some Part-Time Gigs

High school can be a busy time, with homework and extracurricular activities eating up a substantial part of your week. Still, if you can spare just a few hours to work at a coffee shop or retailer, it’ll probably be better for it.

Explore

Open a Bank Account

The venerable piggy bank is a useful savings vehicle for younger children, but when you hit elementary school, consider opening a kids account at an actual bank. It’s a good way to learning the importance of gradually building up your own balance, and it gives you an introduction to the banking industry.

Explore

Make a Habit of Saving

Learning to save isn’t just an essential money habit. Saving teaches discipline and delayed gratification. Saving teaches goal-setting and planning. Saving stresses being prepared. Saving builds security and independence.
